## Verdict — FALSIFIED, with mechanism
- **The SET_SUBSPACE is flat ~0.55–0.62 at EVERY N** and never wins. For the mean-preserving rank-1 change, the
  sample/shrinkage CORRELATION *improves with N* to 0.99; the subspace does not benefit from more samples and
  loses badly at large N. At small N (9), NO method beats ~0.67 — a subtle distributional change is near-
  undetectable from ~9 samples (statistical floor), and the subspace does not extract more signal.
- **There is no "small-sample denoising free lunch."** The subspace's fixed-rank top-k truncation DISCARDS the
  subtle rank-1 change (it sits below the top-k of the material's own high-rank covariance), whereas the
  regularized correlation captures it via its full off-diagonal structure. The truncation is a liability, not a
  denoiser — the same failure mode as material-subspace/orientation.
- Pre-registered gate: "if SET-SUBSPACE never beats shrinkage at small N, Bet 1 dies." It never did, at any N or
  change type, with or without illumination.
