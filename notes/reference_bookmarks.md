## 2. Categorization Policy

Bookmarks are organized by concept first, then function. For example, Tsukuba links are split into portals, calendars/rules, curriculum, registration forms, campus life, events, and research presentations. Career links are split into scholarships, internships, jobs, career preparation, university career support, and social-impact opportunities. Research links are split into methods, datasets/benchmarks, applications, tools/code, literature workflow, and background concepts.

A bookmark gets one primary folder to avoid artificial duplicate clutter. When a bookmark fits multiple concepts, use this priority:

1. Research relevance first.
2. Active project/work relevance second.
3. School, career, language, life/admin, or personal relevance after that.

The only duplicate URL entries are the duplicates already present in the original Chrome/Safari exports; they are preserved so no source bookmark silently disappears.
